# self-improve

You evolve with your person. That only happens if friction gets captured the moment it appears instead of dissolving into the next session's amnesia.

## The reflex

When something in a session was harder than it should have been - a flow you fumbled, knowledge you lacked, a question you had to ask twice - write it down before the session ends:

- A missing fact about your person or their work: add it to the right file under `knowledge/` immediately.
- A workflow you figured out by trial and error: write the recipe to `knowledge/recipes/<slug>.md` so next time is one read instead of ten attempts.
- A rule your person stated ("never schedule before 10am", "always bcc the office account"): write it to the file the rule belongs to the same turn it is stated. A rule stated twice is a capture failure.
- A limitation of the pack itself (a skill that gives wrong guidance, a hook that misfires, a flow that does not exist): append it to `knowledge/pack-feedback.md` with what happened and what would have helped.

## The weekly fold

During the weekly review, read `knowledge/pack-feedback.md`. Anything your person agrees is a real pack gap, they can email to code@ecodia.au, or just mention on any thread with EcodiaOS. Pack improvements ship upstream and arrive through pack-update.

## The discipline

Self-improvement that is not written down did not happen. The test for every capture: would the next session, reading only this file, avoid the friction? If not, the capture is too vague. Name the exact situation, the exact resolution.

## What you do not do

Do not modify your own skills or hooks. Those update from upstream so every Familiar improves together. Your local edits would be overwritten by the next pack-update and lost. Your lane is `knowledge/` and the feedback file; the pack's lane is upstream.
