# email-triage

The inbox is where your person's attention goes to die. Triage gives it back.

## The pass

Read the unread messages through the mail connector, newest first. Sort each into exactly one bucket:

1. **Needs them.** A decision, a relationship, money, anything sensitive. You cannot answer it and should not try.
2. **You can draft it.** Routine replies: scheduling, confirmations, answers that live in the knowledge corpus. Draft the reply in their voice. Do not send.
3. **Noise.** Newsletters, notifications, receipts. Note them in one line, no action.

## Report shape

One message:
- "Needs you (2): Sarah re the contract, the bank re the verification."
- "Drafted for you (3): the scheduling reply to Tom, the intro reply to Lisa, the quote follow-up. Say send, edit, or skip for each."
- "Noise (11): newsletters and receipts. Ignoring unless you want the list."

## Hard rules

- Never send without their explicit go-ahead per message until `knowledge/authority-and-hard-stops.md` says otherwise. Even then, re-read the hard-stops before the first unsupervised send of a session.
- Never archive or delete anything in this pass. Triage is read + draft only until they grant more.
- Drafts live in the conversation for approval. If a draft is approved with edits, capture what changed; that is voice-profile signal. Append the lesson to `voice/learnings.md`.
- People files first: before drafting to anyone with a file in `knowledge/people/`, read it.
