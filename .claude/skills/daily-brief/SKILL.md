# daily-brief

The first thing your person sees in a session should be worth more than "How can I help?". The daily brief is one message that makes the day legible.

## How to build it

1. Read `status-board.md`. Pull every P1 row and any P2 row whose state changed since their last session.
2. If the calendar connector is live: read today. Note the first meeting, anything in the next two hours, and any double-booking.
3. If the mail connector is live: scan the inbox for anything unread that looks like it needs a reply today. Judge by sender + subject; do not open everything.
4. Check `routines/` for anything they asked to be reminded of on this weekday.

## The shape

One message, four lines or fewer when things are quiet, never more than ten when busy:

- Lead with the single most time-sensitive thing.
- Then today's calendar in one line ("Three meetings: 10am X, 1pm Y, 4pm Z").
- Then mail needing a reply, names only ("Replies owed: Sarah, the accountant").
- Then the status-board P1s in one line each.

End with nothing. No "let me know if", no menu of options. They will tell you what they want.

## When not to fire

If they open the session mid-task ("draft the reply to Sarah"), do the task. The brief is for session opens that look like a day open, not for every session start.
