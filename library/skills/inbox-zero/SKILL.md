# inbox-zero

email-triage handles the daily pass. inbox-zero is the bigger job: a full backlog driven to empty in one sitting, every message accounted for.

## When to use

- They have a backlog and want it dealt with in one go, not the daily skim.
- Returning from leave to a wall of unread.
- They explicitly ask to get to zero.

## When NOT to use

- The routine daily pass. Use email-triage for that; it is lighter.
- When they only have ten minutes. inbox-zero is a focused session; name that up front.

## The method

Work oldest-first or newest-first, but commit to one direction and clear every message. For each, choose exactly one of four fates:

1. **Reply now**: anything you can answer from the knowledge corpus or a routine pattern. Draft it in their voice. Read the person file first if it is addressed to someone with one.
2. **Needs them**: a decision, money, a relationship, anything sensitive. Pull it into a short "needs you" list with one line each.
3. **Archive**: noise, receipts, newsletters, resolved threads. Move them out (only once they have granted archive authority; until then, list them for a bulk archive they approve).
4. **Queue with a date**: anything that needs action later. Note it on the status board or set a reminder per the contact-followup skill, then archive it so it stops sitting in the inbox as a worry.

## Report shape

When the pass is done, one message:

- "Cleared 84 messages. Drafted 9 replies (listed below, say send/edit/skip). 4 need you (listed). Queued 6 with dates. Archived 65."
- The drafted-replies list and the needs-you list, each one line.

## Hard rules

- Nothing gets sent without their per-message go-ahead until the hard-stops say otherwise.
- Nothing important gets archived on assumption. If you are not sure a thread is resolved, it goes to "needs them", not to archive.
- Every queued item gets a real trigger (a status-board row or a reminder), not a mental note. A queue with no date is just a slower inbox.
- Read `knowledge/people/` files before drafting to anyone who has one.
