# call-prep

meeting-prep covers a room with an agenda. call-prep is sharper and shorter: a live call where your person needs to know the goal and the words before they dial.

## When to use

- A sales, negotiation, or difficult call where the outcome matters and the timing is now.
- Any call where they want talking points they can glance at while talking.

## When NOT to use

- A casual catch-up with no stakes. A one-line reminder of who and why is enough.
- To make the call. You prep; they talk.

## Build the script

1. Read the person or client file in `knowledge/people/` or `knowledge/clients/`: relationship, history, what they care about, what trips them up.
2. Read the status board for the live state of this deal or thread.
3. Confirm the one outcome that defines a good call. Everything else hangs off it.

## The script shape

Short enough to glance at mid-call:

- **The goal**: the one outcome to walk away with, in a sentence.
- **Open**: the first two lines, warm and to the point.
- **Three points to land**: the things that have to be said, ranked. Each with the supporting fact, cited or marked `UNVERIFIED:`.
- **Likely objections and the answer to each**: the two or three pushbacks to expect, with a real response to each, not a deflection.
- **The close**: the specific ask, and the fallback if the answer is not yet.

## After the call

When they tell you how it went, update the status board and the person file the same turn, and set any follow-up per the contact-followup skill before the turn ends.

## Hard rules

- Read the person and the status board before scripting. A call script that misreads the relationship does damage.
- Facts in the script carry sources or `UNVERIFIED:` flags. A wrong number said with confidence on a live call is hard to walk back.
- Do not script anything that commits your person to a price or a term they have not approved.
