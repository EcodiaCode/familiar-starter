# content-repurpose

Your person already did the hard part by making the thing. Repurposing gets ten outputs from one input without ten times the work.

## When to use

- They have a finished piece (a talk, an article, a long post, a recorded call) and want more formats out of it.
- A piece that performed well and is worth reshaping for another channel.

## When NOT to use

- Source material that is not theirs to reuse, or that contains anything confidential. Check first.
- To publish anything. You produce the variants; posting is gated to their approval and the channel rules in the cdp-usage skill.

## The work

1. Read the source piece fully. Find the two or three ideas that actually carry it.
2. Read `voice/profile.md` so every variant sounds like them, not like a content mill.
3. Produce the formats they asked for, or propose the ones that fit:
   - **Short posts**: each one idea, standing on its own, no "as I said in my talk" framing.
   - **A newsletter section**: longer, warmer, with the throughline restored.
   - **A thread or carousel**: one idea per beat, a clear first line that earns the read.
   - **A pull-quote set**: the lines worth lifting verbatim.
4. Respect each channel's shape: length limits, whether images are needed, how that platform actually reads. Drive the live surface per the cdp-usage skill rather than quoting limits from memory.

## Deliver

- Show every variant inline in the chat, labelled by format.
- For anything they want scheduled, set it up per the cdp-usage skill's routine path, and confirm the schedule took before claiming it is queued.

## Hard rules

- Read the voice profile first. Repurposed content that sounds generic defeats the point.
- No invented claims slipped in to pad a post. If the source did not say it, it does not go in.
- Nothing publishes without their go-ahead until they set a standing rule for a channel.
- Never claim a post is scheduled without reading the schedule back.
